Quick Reference Glossary

Key Investing Terms Explained

Click any term for a full AI-powered explanation with examples.

P/E Ratio Valuation
Price-to-earnings ratio measures how much investors pay per dollar of company profit. A lower P/E can signal value; a higher one may signal growth expectations.
Market Cap Size
Total value of all a company's shares. Small-cap (<$2B), Mid-cap ($2–10B), Large-cap (>$10B). Larger caps are generally more stable.
Dividend Yield Income
Annual dividend payment as a percentage of stock price. A 3% yield means you earn $3/year for every $100 invested, paid quarterly.
Beta Risk
Measures a stock's volatility vs. the market. Beta of 1 = moves with market. >1 = more volatile. <1 = more stable than the market.
ETF Instrument
Exchange-Traded Fund. A basket of stocks you buy like a single stock. Great for instant diversification at low cost.
Dollar-Cost Averaging Strategy
Investing a fixed amount regularly regardless of price. Reduces impact of market timing and smooths out volatility over time.
Compound Interest Growth
Earning returns on your returns. $10,000 at 10%/year becomes $25,937 in 10 years — the core of why starting early matters so much.
Analyst Rating Research
Professional Wall Street analysts rate stocks: Strong Buy, Buy, Hold, Underperform, Sell. Consensus of 20–40 analysts carries meaningful signal.
52-Week High/Low Price
The highest and lowest price a stock has traded over the past year. Context for whether a stock is near highs (momentum) or lows (potential value).
